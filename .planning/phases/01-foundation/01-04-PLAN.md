    **2. Create `docs/AI-CONTENT-POLICY.md`:**

    This document establishes the copyright guardrails and content generation standards for Phase 2.

    Required sections:

    a) **Purpose** — This policy governs all AI-generated content in the project. It MUST be referenced in every AI prompt used for content generation.

    b) **Permitted Philosophical Traditions** — Content may synthesize from these traditions as PHILOSOPHICAL SCHOOLS, not as specific copyrighted texts:
       - Advaita Vedanta (associated with Adi Shankaracharya)
       - Vishishtadvaita (associated with Ramanujacharya)
       - Bhakti traditions (broad devotional perspective)

    c) **Paraphrase Requirement** — All AI-generated content MUST:
       - Synthesize philosophical positions in original language
       - Attribute ideas to traditions (e.g., "The Advaita tradition holds..."), not to specific modern books
       - Never reproduce verbatim or near-verbatim text from any published commentary
       - Use the attribution format: "AI-generated synthesis of [tradition] philosophical tradition based on public domain sources"

    d) **Prohibited Sources** — The following copyrighted works MUST NOT be reproduced, quoted, or closely paraphrased:
       - Bhaktivedanta Book Trust (BBT/ISKCON): Prabhupada's Bhagavad Gita As It Is translations and purports
       - Chinmaya Mission: Swami Chinmayananda's Holy Geeta commentary
       - Sri Aurobindo Ashram: Essays on the Gita
       - Any commentary by a 20th-21st century author still under copyright

    e) **Transliteration Standard** — IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration) is the ONLY accepted standard. Harvard-Kyoto, ITRANS, and other romanization schemes are not accepted. IAST uses diacritical marks: ā, ī, ū, ṛ, ṝ, ḷ, ṃ, ḥ, ṅ, ñ, ṭ, ḍ, ṇ, ś, ṣ.

    f) **Verse Numbering** — All verse references use the Gita Press (Gorakhpur) 700-verse, 18-chapter structure. See docs/SOURCES.md for the canonical verse count table.

    g) **Legal Basis** — Delhi High Court (2024) ruling: original Sanskrit religious scriptures are public domain. Specific translations, commentaries, and purports by modern authors are copyrighted separately.

    Keep the document clear, direct, and actionable. It will be referenced in AI prompts during Phase 2 content generation.
